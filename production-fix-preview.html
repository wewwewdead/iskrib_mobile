Production UI Direction

Make Iskrib feel like one product again.

This preview turns the audit into a concrete direction: fewer competing actions, stronger editorial hierarchy, tighter profile personalization, and a refined onboarding flow that feels premium without turning setup into a form-heavy wizard.

Refined setup, then creation
One dominant action per screen
Editorial Explore, not feed-plus-rankings
Curated personalization only

Core Product Rules

1
Subtraction firstHome and onboarding stop competing with themselves. The product should feel quiet, not crowded.
2
Accent means priorityGold and amber are reserved for the action or signal that matters most in the moment.
3
Identity over utilityProfile leads with the writer, not with dashboard shortcuts fighting for attention.
4
Distinct surfacesExplore, Home, and Profile each get a different job and a different rhythm.
Onboarding
Calm setup with fewer steps

Users still get an onboarding flow, but it is tighter, warmer, and more intentional than the current multi-card sequence.

Home
One calm feed

Search, presence, and feed controls stay, but in a quieter order with less UI competition.

Explore
Editorial curation

Discovery becomes a magazine-like layer with themes, lists, and highlighted writers.

Profile
Authored identity

Profiles stay expressive, but the brand stays in control of what expressive means.

What This Preview Is Solving

Hierarchy

The app stops yelling with every component. Each screen has one job and one obvious next move.

Consistency

Primary actions use the dark ink button treatment, while gold becomes signal rather than wallpaper.

Brand Confidence

The literary identity gets stronger because the product is more restrained, not because it adds more decoration.
